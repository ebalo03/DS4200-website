More on Mardi Gras

Mardi Gras, which means "Fat Tuesday" in French, is a famous annual celebration held primarily in New Orleans, Louisiana. It marks the culmination of Carnival season, a period of feasting and revelry before the season of Lent in Christianity. Mardi Gras is a lively, colorful festival known for its parades, balls, costumes, and vibrant street parties.

The festivities officially begin on Twelfth Night (January 6) and continue through Fat Tuesday, the day before Ash Wednesday. The highlight is the grand parades, where krewes (organized groups) decorate elaborate floats and throw beads, coins, and other trinkets to the crowds. People wear costumes, often donning masks and feathers, and participate in spirited dances.

Fun Facts

  1. Colors: The official colors of Mardi Gras are purple (justice), green (faith), and gold (power).
  2. King Cake: A traditional pastry eaten during Mardi Gras, often containing a small figurine; the person who finds it is expected to host the next party.
  3. Beads & Throws: Parade-goers collect beads and other throws, symbolizing the connection between the krewes and the crowd.
  4. Carnival Season: Mardi Gras is part of a larger Carnival season celebrated worldwide, especially in places like Brazil and Italy.
  5. Rex: The Rex parade is one of the oldest and most famous Mardi Gras parades.
